01 · About

Adrian König is a Ph.D. candidate at the Institute for Private International Law and Civil Procedure (CIVPRO) at the Faculty of Law of the University of Bern, supervised by Prof. Dr. Florian Eichel. His research focuses on data-science-driven litigation and examines how quantitative methods can support tactical and strategic decision-making in civil procedure.

He runs an independent Zurich-based practice for legal research and project support serving B2B clients and is currently preparing for the written part of the Zurich bar examination.

Through academic and practical training across law, business, finance, economics, and technology, he has developed a deliberately broad and interdisciplinary skill set. He combines analytical precision with resilience and discipline shaped by elite sport, as well as a strong entrepreneurial drive.

Outside his professional work, Adrian primarily trains in mixed martial arts, kickboxing, and CrossFit. In winter, he can usually be found on freestyle skis in snowparks, while his trips abroad often revolve around surfing. He also occasionally plays DJ sets.

02 · Research & Projects

In his current research, König approaches civil procedure from a tactical angle, using quantitative methods to examine where, when, and how information advantages arise in litigation and how they can contribute to more sustainable procedural outcomes. Beyond this core focus, he works on a broader range of legal and interdisciplinary questions and has published across several fields.

03 · Experience

König runs an independent Zurich-based practice for legal research and project support, advising selected B2B clients on matters requiring both legal analysis and quantitative methods. He also teaches legal subjects as a lecturer for Delta Group AG in Zurich. As of September 2026, he will join the Institute for Private International Law and Civil Procedure (CIVPRO) at the Faculty of Law of the University of Bern as a research associate at the chair of Prof. Dr. Florian Eichel.

Previously, he worked as a litigation trainee with an internationally recognised dispute resolution specialist and gained experience with several leading Swiss commercial law firms. His broader professional background combines legal practice, academic teaching, project leadership, client-facing instruction, and operational work in demanding environments. This includes his work as president and project lead at the Academic Surf Club Switzerland, as a private-client ski instructor in Laax, and in various assignments for security service providers.
